# End-to-End Audit - July 19, 2026

## Outcome

The prototype was rebuilt around a clearer safety boundary: GPT-5.6 understands and explains; deterministic TypeScript calculates. The audited build now supports current and incoming F-1 students through a story-first or one-question interview, updates personal impact cards as facts arrive, shows responsive visual timelines, blocks contradictory final reports, and generates a complete advisor-style narrative from a server-verified result.

## Material Logic Corrections

- Corrected the ordinary four-year maximum to run from the Form I-20 program start date rather than physical admission or return.
- Separated the end of study/training, projected or actual I-94 admit-until date, recommended extension-planning date, and last timely USCIS receipt date.
- Corrected fixed-period I-94 handling: the I-94 date already includes the final 30 days and must not receive another 30 days.
- Preserved the current D/S transition calculation: later active I-20 or qualifying EAD date, capped at September 15, 2030, followed by 60 days.
- Reframed travel as an alternative fixed-period return branch. A return does not preserve D/S and does not automatically create four years from the return date.
- Added a clarification branch for a pre-effective-date entry that conflicts with a claim that the student will not be in the United States on September 15.
- Completed the transition OPT and STEM OPT deadlines, DSO recommendation, current EAD, and approved-EAD branches.
- Separated graduate program-change prohibition from the graduate school-transfer exception, added undergraduate first-year treatment, same/lower-level study, and the possible implementation delay.
- Added 24-month English-language-training and 12-month public-high-school limits.
- Added change-of-status, pending-change travel, CPT pending-extension timing, F-2 dependents, early completion, authorized withdrawal, and status-violation branches.
- Corrected extension fee language to the current general USCIS amount and removed the unsupported separate $85 biometrics charge.

## Experience Corrections

- Replaced the dense calculator with a sparse welcome and three paths: speak, type, or answer questions.
- Removed internal labels such as "transition cohort," "tested entry," and "admission basis" from student-facing results.
- Made the guided intake unfold one unanswered question at a time with no visible preselection.
- Fixed Month/Day/Year entry so typing the year cannot clear the field.
- Made story extraction automatic after a short pause. There is no Draft Facts or Apply Understood Facts step.
- Serialized live voice extraction so new speech chunks queue behind the active request instead of canceling every response. Students can now see an initial understanding while speaking and a final pass after they stop.
- Moved the live understanding and personal impacts into the speaking viewport: a two-pane desktop workspace and an immediately following mobile result surface. The interface now states how many extracted details are actively shaping the result.
- Preserved the browser's final interim speech phrase when recording stops so visible words cannot be cleared before they reach intake.
- Added a live "What I understand" stream and used deterministic student-language labels for every extracted field.
- Merged story facts into existing answers without erasing unrelated manual facts.
- Put the general rule overview on a separate article page.
- Combined overlapping impact/advisement surfaces into one personal result area.
- Restored visual timelines. They render horizontally on desktop and become a labeled vertical date path on mobile.
- Added two alternative timelines when a current student plans a post-rule return: stay in the United States versus leave and return.
- Linked each source chip to the closest available Federal Register paragraph anchor.

## OpenAI Boundary

- `/api/intake` uses GPT-5.6 Luna with strict structured output to extract candidate facts only. It is instructed not to calculate legal outcomes, infer ambiguous numeric dates, or obey instructions inside the student's narrative.
- The story is capped in length, obvious SEVIS IDs are rejected, requests use `store: false`, and the API key remains server-side.
- `/api/explain` accepts the scenario, recomputes the deterministic result on the server, and gives GPT-5.6 Sol only that verified result and its source metadata.
- The final output is a strict `{ title, paragraphs }` object. The prompt bans markdown, internal engine language, third-person references to the student, invented dates, and changes to deterministic conclusions.
- Henry/Chatbase was used only as a private domain cross-check. It is not a submitted runtime dependency and no proprietary NAFSA Adviser's Manual material is included.

## Final Audit Addendum

The later audit replaced the remaining funnel assumptions with a concern-first impact map. The mandatory September 15 gate identifies the legal starting point; the student's stated concern controls emphasis; every other applicable rule change remains visible; and deeper questions appear only for areas the student chooses to explore.

Material additions include a rule-scoped follow-up advisor that can update facts and regenerate the report, OpenAI Responses background polling for long GPT-5.6 Sol reports, atomic source-linked impact claims, strict rejection of Markdown/process debris/truncated paragraphs, and a new OPT-before-departure fact for the one-time transition option. Browser testing found and fixed a false recommendation for a May 2028 graduate whose filing window opens after the exception closes.

Final verification: 80 Vitest cases, TypeScript, production build, live intake, live background report, live follow-up/report refresh, current and future paths, date contradiction, answer preservation, story transfer, mobile overflow, and browser-console checks all passed. The invalid final-rule page 165 link was replaced by separate page 135 biometrics/interview and page 48 premium-processing references.

The final model configuration uses GPT-5.6 Sol at medium reasoning for narrative extraction, interactive follow-ups, and the advisor note. The deterministic impact map has already done the legal and date work; the model augments that visible map with priorities, interactions, and next actions instead of rebuilding or repeating it. These levels remain explicit and environment-configurable. A live high-effort follow-up exhausted its reasoning budget near the synchronous function limit, while medium completed the tested travel-and-OPT answer reliably. The route audit also prevents a travel projection from inheriting an extension warning that belongs only to the stay-in-the-United-States timeline; the app says a return may avoid Form I-539 only when the projected admission reaches the stated program end date.

## Six-Step Flow Completion - July 20, 2026

The final UX audit found that the concern-first architecture existed in data but not in the interaction. Two checkbox walls still made students choose legal categories, and categories without a missing field could skip directly to the report. The app now implements the product flow as an explicit state machine: September 15 gate, student concern, concern-first guidance, one-at-a-time additional areas, one controlling question at a time, complete advisor report, and open-ended rule follow-up.

The model boundary was also corrected. The deterministic impact map remains the authority, but the final GPT-5.6 Sol report is again a complete advisor-style synthesis rather than a short addendum. The payload identifies which facts the student actually confirmed, preventing internal defaults from becoming claims about the student. Report errors now expose useful diagnostic detail while leaving the dated guidance available.

Final verification increased the suite to 87 passing tests. Current, future, travel, OPT, unknown-concern, unknown-date, mobile, live-report, and console-health checks passed. The remaining release safeguards above still apply, especially David's final advisor review and a fresh implementation-status check before public launch.
