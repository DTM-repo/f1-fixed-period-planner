# Build Week Demo Video

Target length: **2:35-2:50**. The submission must remain under three minutes.

## Recording Recommendation

Use **Screen Studio** if its installation and recording permissions take less than 15 minutes. Its automatic zoom, cursor treatment, and simple editing are the fastest route to a polished product demo. Screen Studio requires an active plan to export.

The best free fallback is already on the Mac: press **Shift-Command-5**, record a selected 16:9 portion with the microphone enabled, and trim the result in iMovie. OBS is free and has no watermark, but learning and configuring it today would cost more time than it saves.

Official references:

- [Screen Studio guide](https://screen.studio/guide)
- [Screen Studio download and export information](https://screen.studio/download)
- [Apple screen-recording instructions](https://support.apple.com/en-us/102618)
- [iMovie](https://www.apple.com/imovie/)
- [OBS](https://obsproject.com/help)

## Before Recording

1. Use the deployed site, not localhost.
2. Set the browser to a clean 16:9 window, roughly 1440 by 810, at 100% zoom.
3. Turn on Do Not Disturb and close personal tabs, email, notifications, and password managers.
4. Start a new case and rehearse this exact story once:

   > I am a current undergraduate F-1 student. My I-20 ends May 30, 2027. I want to do OPT, and I plan to travel home in December 2026 and return January 10, 2027.

5. Open the exact-rule citation, create the advisement, and open Explore further once before the recording so there are no surprises.
6. Record without music. Clear voice and readable product audio are enough.

## Script

### 0:00-0:18 - The Problem

**On screen:** Let the black opening play, then reveal the actions.

**Say:**

> On July 17, DHS replaced duration of status with fixed periods of admission for F-1 students. The rule is long, new, and intensely personal: travel, OPT, a program change, or one date can completely change the answer. I built F-1 Duration Mapper so a student can understand their own path without first learning the regulation.

### 0:18-0:50 - Natural-Language Intake

**On screen:** Choose voice or typing, enter the prepared story, and show the confirmation bullets.

**Say:**

> A student starts in their own words. GPT-5.6 Luna extracts only the facts and concerns that matter, while keeping separate events separate: the current program, OPT, travel, and any later program. The student sees exactly what was understood and can correct it before any result is calculated.

### 0:50-1:28 - One Question at a Time

**On screen:** Confirm the September 15 answer and complete one or two controlling questions. Let the cards and timeline change.

**Say:**

> From there, the app asks one question at a time. It addresses the student's concern first, but it also identifies every other part of this rule that could affect someone in their categories. As answers arrive, a deterministic TypeScript engine updates the conclusion, warnings, and dates. In this case, the planned return triggers the new fixed-date rules, and the OPT filing window and March 18 transition deadline appear on the timeline.

### 1:28-1:52 - Sources and Advisement

**On screen:** Open a citation sheet, briefly show the highlighted Federal Register passage, return, and create the advisement.

**Say:**

> Every legal conclusion is tied to a verified source. The citation opens the closest Federal Register paragraph and tells the student what language to look for. For the final advisement, the server recalculates the case and gives GPT-5.6 Sol only the verified facts, dates, consequences, and citations. The model explains the result; it does not invent the law.

### 1:52-2:15 - Locked Result and Explorer

**On screen:** Show the headed report, timeline, PDF action, lock notice, and Explore further page.

**Say:**

> The finished report is a locked snapshot that can be saved as a formatted PDF. The student can explore another applicable issue or ask a rule-scoped follow-up without silently changing that report. If a new fact changes the case, the app requires an explicit revised advisement.

### 2:15-2:45 - How Codex Helped

**On screen:** Stay on the product, slowly moving across the timeline and result. Optionally show the README's collaboration section for five seconds.

**Say:**

> I brought fifteen years of international-student advising and current immigration-law study. Codex helped turn that expertise into the rule engine, the temporal case model, and 124 regression tests. It also replayed real student cases in the browser and caught failures such as an EAD date being confused with a program date and a past trip entering a future-travel branch. Together, Codex, GPT-5.6, and deterministic verification closed the distance from a new Federal Register rule to a usable student tool in days.

### 2:45-2:52 - Close

**On screen:** Return to the report headline or opening wordmark.

**Say:**

> F-1 Duration Mapper turns a complicated rule into one student's clear, sourced next step.
